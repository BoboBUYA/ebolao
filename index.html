ℹ️ Virus Profile & Response

🦠 Bundibugyo Virus (BVD)

One of 6 known Ebola species. First identified in Uganda 2007. Only 3rd detected outbreak ever (Uganda 2007–08, DRC 2012, DRC/Uganda 2026). CFR in previous outbreaks: 25–40%. No licensed vaccine or specific treatment. Existing countermeasures (rVSV-ZEBOV, Inmazeb, Ebanga) all target the Zaire strain — cross-protection not established.

🚧 Response Challenges

Active armed conflict in Ituri & Nord-Kivu limits access. 273,000+ displaced persons in Ituri alone. 10 million people in acute hunger across the region. Ituri recorded 11 incidents against humanitarian actors Jan–Mar 2026. No ring vaccination possible — no approved BVD vaccine. Response relies on classic containment: case isolation, contact tracing, safe burials.

✈️ International Spread Risk

Uganda border with DRC closed 28 May. Canada imposed 90-day entry ban for DRC, Uganda & South Sudan. Kenya court halted a 50-bed US Ebola facility planned in Nairobi. Kampala is a major air hub — WHO warns of risk of international spread. CDC Travel Notice: Level 3 (DRC), Level 1 (Uganda). WHO DG warns of "catastrophic collision" of disease and conflict.

🧬 Symptoms

Fever · Generalised body pain · Weakness · Vomiting · Diarrhoea · Haemorrhagic manifestations in severe cases. Incubation: 2–21 days. Transmission: direct contact with blood/body fluids of symptomatic cases or deceased.

🏥 WHO Response Actions

Deployment of rapid response teams · Medical supplies delivery · Strengthened surveillance & lab confirmation · IPC assessments · Safe treatment centres · Community engagement · Cross-border preparedness with Uganda. IHR Emergency Committee convened 19 May. Candidate medical countermeasures R&D activated.

📊 Historical Perspective

Since 1976: ~35,000 total Ebola cases across 19 countries. The 2014–2016 West Africa outbreak (Zaire strain) accounts for ~80% of all cumulative cases. DRC leads with 17 outbreaks. The 2018–2020 DRC outbreak (Kivu) was the second-largest ever: 3,481 cases, 2,299 deaths. This 2026 BVD outbreak is already the largest Bundibugyo event in history.
